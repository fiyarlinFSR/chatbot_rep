How to write SOP


How to write & implement a great Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

May 21, 2024
























In the high-stakes world of the oil and gas industry, consistency, safety, and efficiency are paramount. One way to achieve these goals is through well-crafted Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). SOPs provide clear, step-by-step instructions on how to perform tasks correctly and consistently.
This blog will guide you through the process of writing and implementing effective SOPs.
So, lets see what is SOP and why do you need?
An SOP is a detailed, written document that describes the regular recurring activities appropriate to quality operations. It provides a set of instructions that an organization uses to ensure that its processes are consistent and carried out in a standard manner.
Improved Efficiency: SOPs eliminate guesswork and wasted time, allowing employees to complete tasks quickly and accurately.
Enhanced Consistency: Everyone follows the same process, leading to consistent results and higher quality outputs.
Reduced Errors: Clear instructions minimize errors and ensure tasks are completed correctly the first time.
Improved Training: SOPs serve as a valuable training tool for new hires and refresher courses for existing employees.
Better Compliance: Documented procedures can help demonstrate adherence to industry regulations or quality standards.
The best SOPs are living documents. They should be adapted to reflect changes in processes, regulations, and technology
Who Needs to Implement SOP Manuals?
In the oil and gas industry, Implementing SOP manuals is crucial for various roles, including exploration teams, drilling and well operations, production teams, health, safety, and environment (HSE) departments, quality assurance and quality control (QA/QC) teams, supply chain and logistics coordinators, emergency response teams, and human resources (HR).
These manuals ensure consistent, safe, and compliant operations by providing standardized procedures for tasks such as geological surveys, drilling, production, safety inspections, quality control, supply chain management, emergency responses, and employee training. This standardization helps mitigate risks, enhance efficiency, and maintain high operational standards.
What is Included in SOP Manuals?
SOX-compliant SOP manuals typically cover:

Guidelines and Requirements: Establishing the basic framework.
Applicability: Defining who and what the SOPs apply to.
Frequency: Outlining how often procedures should be performed.
Governance: Detailing the oversight and management of procedures.
Control Evidence: Providing proof of compliance and control.

Each manual chapter addresses procedures relevant to the oil and gas sector, such as:

QA Inspection
Installation
Field Inspection/Verification
Calibration
Operation and Maintenance

The aim is to ensure the accuracy, repeatability, and validity of measurements, detailing the methods used, the tools, and the qualifications of personnel. Additional chapters may focus on specific measurements, like using orifice meters for gas or LACT/ACT units for liquid measurement, and compliance with Bureau of Land Management (BLM) regulations.
Step-by-step guide for crafting SOP


Identify the need and scope


Begin by clearly stating the purpose of the SOP. Explain why the procedure is necessary and what it aims to achieve.
Pinpoint a specific process that would benefit from a documented procedure. Is it onboarding new clients, processing purchase orders, or handling customer returns?


Gather Necessary Information


Consult Stakeholders: Engage with the individuals who perform or oversee the tasks to understand the detailed steps and requirements.
Review Existing Documents: Look at existing documents, manuals, or guidelines that might contain relevant information.
Regulations and Standards: Ensure the SOP complies with industry regulations and standards.


Structure the SOP


Title Page:
Include the title of the SOP, document ID, version number, date of creation, and the name of the author or responsible person.
Table of Contents:
For longer SOPs, include a table of contents for easy navigation.
Introduction:
Provide an overview, including the purpose, scope, and applicability of the SOP.
Definitions:
Define any specific terms, acronyms, or jargon used in the SOP.
Roles and Responsibilities:
Specify who is responsible for each part of the procedure.
Materials and Equipment:
List any materials, equipment, or resources required.
Procedure:
Outline the steps in a logical sequence, from start to finish. Include details such as:

Step-by-Step Instructions: Clear and concise instructions.
Safety Precautions: Any safety measures to be taken.
Visual Aids: Diagrams, flowcharts, or pictures to illustrate steps.

Documentation and Records:
Describe what records need to be kept and how they should be maintained.
Review and Revision:
Detail the process for reviewing and updating the SOP


Write the SOP


Now, write your SOP to include its purpose, procedures, scope, and responsibilities. For example,

Start with a clear title and an introduction explaining the purpose of the SOP.
Name the person or team responsible for completing the procedure.
List the materials and equipment needed, if applicable.
Detail the procedure step by step, using clear and concise language.
Use bullet points or numbered lists for easy readability.
Incorporate any safety or compliance guidelines relevant to the procedure.
Include screenshots or images to illustrate complex steps.
Name the person responsible for signing off on the completed procedure.



Review and Revise SOP


Once complete writing the SOP, send it to team members for review.
Internal Review: Have the draft reviewed by stakeholders, supervisors, and subject matter experts for accuracy and completeness.
Testing: Conduct a trial run of the SOP with a small group to ensure it works as intended and is easy to follow.
Revise: Based on feedback from the review and testing, make necessary revisions.
Note: The document should only proceed to the final step after all stakeholders accept and approve the SOP.


Train and Implement the SOP


Once current stakeholders approve the SOP document.
Develop a training plan: Train employees on the new SOP.
Make the SOP readily accessible: Store it electronically or in a central location where everyone can easily find it.
Communicate the SOP rollout: Inform employees about the new SOP and its importance.


Monitoring and Reviewing 


Review the SOP regularly: Schedule periodic reviews to ensure it stays current with any changes in processes, regulations, or technology.
Encourage feedback: Allow employees to provide suggestions for improvement.
By following these steps, you can create and implement a great SOP that will improve efficiency, ensure consistency, and promote quality within your organization.
