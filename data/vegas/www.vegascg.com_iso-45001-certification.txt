ISO 45001:2018 Certification
ISO 45001 Certification is an international standard establishing requirement for occupational health and safety management systems, including guidance for application of the standard. The ISO 45001 standard defines strategies for organizations to boost their Occupational Health and Safety (OH&S) performance by taking proactive steps to stop employee injuries and poor health and supply a secure and healthy workplace.
The ISO 45001 includes sections addressing the context of a company, and for designing, supporting, in operation and evaluating a sturdy OH&S management system. ISO 45001:2018 standard utilizes the concept of PDCA (Plan, Do, Check, Act) method that has been used for many years to boost product and method quality. Moreover, ISO 45001 Certification provides guidelines for the organization to create a safe and effective work place for employees and others.
ISO 45001 Certification applies to all organizations, regardless of any size, type, and field of activity. The ISO 45001:2018 certification inspires the top management to develop a safe culture and to reduce the operational risks in the management process and services. Achieving ISO 45001 Certification improves employee satisfaction and therefore increases workflow efficiency.

FAQ







 What is ISO 45001 Certification? 



ISO 45001:2018 is an international standard that specifies requirements for an occupational health and safety management system. The ISO 45001 standard enables organizations to proactively improve OH&S performance in preventing injury and ill-health.





 How to Get ISO 45001 Certification?

Perform initial OH&S review gap analysis

Create a plan

Review the system

Get certified





 What Are The Key Features of ISO 45001:2018? 



Key elements are Risk Management, Operational Controls, Incident Management, and Emergency Preparedness, etc. Detailed policies and procedures in accordance with ISO 45001 standard that helps organizations to practice good health and safety management system.





 How is ISO 45001 Different from OHSAS 18001? 



Structure similar to other management system standards
Top-down approach to safety
More focus on prevention than control
New clause for documented information
Ensure suppliers and contractors manage risks





 How Much Does ISO 45001 Certification Cost? 



Pricing of certification depends on the size of the organization, business scope, and projects or resources deployed as it determines the number of days required for audits. Based on this and the man-day rate of the chosen certification body, the prices vary.
